Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France, was built between 1887 and 1889 for the Worldâ€™s Fair to celebrate the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, the tower stands 324 meters tall and was the tallest structure in the world at the time of its completion. Made of iron and weighing over 10,000 tons, it has become one of the most famous landmarks in the world.

Today, the Eiffel Tower attracts millions of visitors every year and is considered a symbol of Paris and French culture.
