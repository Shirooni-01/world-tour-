Leaning Tower

The Leaning Tower of Pisa in Italy is famous for its unique tilt, caused by soft ground that couldn’t support its weight during construction in 1173. Standing about 56 meters tall, it serves as the bell tower of Pisa’s cathedral. After many restoration efforts, the tower has been stabilized and is now a popular tourist attraction known worldwide.

Today, it is one of the most iconic monuments in the world and a major tourist attraction.
